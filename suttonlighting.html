LED replacement schemes

Most lighting in the borough was replaced during 2013-2017, on main roads using Teceo 1 and 2s and Axia 1s and later Amperas. Side roads were replaced with Axia 1s. These replaced mostly SOX but also SON and CPO. Afterwards replacements on side roads gradually continued with Axia 1s. However, there was still a decent amount of SOX surviving from the 1970s and 1980s. However, in 2021 VolkerHighways were appointed with a 10 year maintenance scheme. 2024 was the death year for most remaining non-LED lighting, with virtually all Sapphires replaced by May 2024 and the SOX rapidly been replaced as well, with Axia 3s and Axia 3 Evos. I will be visiting these in August 2024, and images and reports will arrive then.

Sutton Council

Sutton mostly has Axias, Teceos, Amperas, Axia 3 Evos, Albanies, and possibly P224s, P226s, Z9454s, and other SOX. Some of this includes some rare survivors.

History

I believe Sutton was orignally lit with ESLA GLS lanterns, but replacement of these started in the early 1970s. Suprisingly, some survived till 2002. The SOX which started replacing the eslas in the 1970s was originally mostly Phosco P224s and GEC Z9454s, although GEC Z9536s were also sometimes used. Oddly, the P224s (mostly) run 35W SOX, even though they are designed for 55W SOX. And similarly, the GEC Z9454s (mostly) run 55W SOX, even though they were designed for 90W SOX (although I believe it was originally designed for SO/H or possibly SOI/H). Why this is i'm not sure but they probably weren't like that originally. Before LED "upgrades", main roads usually had Iridiums, but what was there before then I don't know, but possibly GEC Z9554Ms. In the early-mid 2010s, this stock started to be upgraded to LED (mostly with Axias and Teceos but also Amperas).
